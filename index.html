Every commercial strategy depends on three questions.

Most can't. Signal is built to answer them — systematically, from your actual customer data, every quarter.

Question 01
Who are your best customers, and why did they choose you?
Not your biggest customers — your best ones. The ones who stay, expand, and refer others. Signal identifies who they are and what predicted them so you can find more of them.
Question 02
Which priorities actually serve those customers?
Most roadmaps are built on internal assumptions. Signal surfaces what your best customers actually care about and where you are investing in things that don't move the needle for them.
Question 03
What keeps them from leaving?
By the time a customer tells you they are leaving, the decision was made months earlier. Signal identifies the signals that predict churn before they are obvious so you can act while it still matters.

The platform

Six modules. One shared intelligence layer.

Each module runs on its own or alongside the others. Customer insight feeds customer profiling. Customer profiling feeds strategy validation. Every output becomes the next input, so the intelligence compounds rather than sitting in separate tools.

Module 01 — Entry module
Customer Insight Synthesizer
Upload interviews, surveys, reviews, and support data. Signal extracts the themes your customers keep coming back to, scores them by frequency and emotional intensity, and maps each one to the three questions.
Rapidly synthesize customer insight to uncover revenue opportunities, product gaps, and service misses — so your team spends less time guessing and more time acting on what customers are actually telling you.
Module 02 — Builds on 01
Best Customer Profile
Combines your customer insight themes with your account data to find the customers who generate disproportionate value. Signal identifies the attributes and signals that predicted them before they became your best customers.
Shift sales and marketing toward accounts most likely to become long-term, high-value relationships. Most companies discover a segment they were underinvesting in entirely. The best acquisition strategy starts with knowing who your best customers already are.
Module 03 — Builds on 02
Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) Stress-Tester
Compares who your company says it is targeting against who your data shows actually generates the most value. Surfaces the gaps between your stated strategy and commercial reality, ranked by revenue impact.
Most companies are targeting the wrong customers and don't know it. Every wasted sales cycle, every poor-fit acquisition, every high-cost renewal fight traces back to an ICP that hasn't been tested against real data.
Module 04 — Builds on 01 + 02
Synthetic Customer Personas
Builds a queryable model of your best customer type from real insight themes and account patterns. Ask it how your best customer would respond to a pricing change, a new product feature, or a shift in messaging before you commit to anything.
Test pricing, messaging, and product decisions against a real customer model rather than internal assumptions. Research that used to take months and significant budget now takes hours.
Module 05 — Builds on 01
Churn Signal Detector
Monitors your account base against the retention patterns identified in your customer research. Flags at-risk accounts early, tells you whether the risk is worth acting on based on customer fit, and recommends the right intervention for the type of churn showing up.
Know which accounts need attention months before renewal. Know which churns are worth preventing and which are not. And know that when the account manager handling this relationship leaves, the intelligence stays.
Module 06 — Aggregation layer
Intelligence Brief
Pulls everything Signal knows about an account or segment into a single on-demand brief. Generated fresh each time, it surfaces the risk signals, the relevant customer insight, the relationship health, and the single most important thing to address right now.
Walk into every client conversation better prepared than the person across the table. Your team stops relying on gut feel and starts making account decisions from intelligence that survives turnover, compounds over time, and reflects what is actually happening.
